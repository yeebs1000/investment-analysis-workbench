Costs, plateaus, robustness, drawdown

  • Cost stress: at 2.5% round-trip slippage PF 1.42 / +16.0%; at 5% PF 1.29 / +11.5%; breakeven ≈ 11%; at 15% the strategy loses. The edge survives realistic costs but not sloppy execution — liquid, tight-spread underlyings only.
  • Parameter plateau: every neighbor of the tuned point (drift 4/12, delta .25/.35, DTE 25/45) earns +20.1% to +23.4% — a smooth plateau, no curve-fit cliff. DTE 45 tested mildly better; left alone on the plateau principle.
  • Robustness: no year supplies more than 31% of P&L, top symbol 1.9%, top five 9.0%. Honest flags: Long Straddle contributes 48.5% of P&L and the top 5% of trades supply 55% — a long-vol, tail-driven profit engine that deserves its own ask-side-fill validation before real money.
  • Drawdown (scale-invariant): max drawdown ≈ 2.2–2.8× the standing total-risk cap; longest underwater stretch 385 days at any size; 26% of months negative. At a 5% total-risk cap: −13.5% MDD, Calmar ~3.4, ~+22% CAGR after realistic slippage. Marks at expiry only, so true intra-trade MDD is deeper.
  • Honest portfolio Sharpe: 0.57 on non-overlapping cohorts (the 1.47 overlapping figure is the optimistic bound).

Confidence, along four axes

A single number would flatter the result. Confidence is high on some dimensions and genuinely limited on others — and the limited ones are structural, not fixable with more data.

Statistical significance

High

60,954 S&P trades; win rate holds at +6.9σ after a conservative overlap haircut, and a symbol-block bootstrap puts the return-on-risk interval at [+19.2%, +24.1%] — profitability itself, not just win rate, is clear of zero.

Over-fitting risk

Very low

Now evidenced, not just argued: 110% out-of-sample retention, every lever valid on in-sample data alone, and a smooth parameter plateau (all neighbors +20–23%). Curve-fit strategies do not do this.

Regime coverage

Moderate

~93% of trades fall in bull regimes. The bull result is deeply sampled; the bear result (~4k trades) is positive and significant but rests on one 2022-era stretch — thinner evidence.

Transfer to live P&L

Limited, now bounded

Premiums are still modeled, but the cost stress bounds the exposure: the edge survives 5% round-trip slippage (+11.5%) and dies at ~11%. A daily recorder now archives real chains (109 symbols, sector-stratified) — the path to closing this axis with paper-fill comparison.

What this report does not prove

The single biggest caveat: no historical option chains exist for this account, so entry premiums are priced by Black-Scholes off trailing realized volatility. Any edge that lives in real implied vol vs realized — a large part of live options P&L — is assumed away here.
  • No transaction costs. Commissions, bid-ask slippage, and early-assignment risk are unmodeled; real net returns sit below these figures.
  • Hold-to-expiry marks. The profit-taking and stop rules aren't simulated — conservative for premium sellers, generous for debit buyers.
  • Bull-heavy sample. The window is dominated by a rising market; sustained-bear behaviour is thinly tested.
  • In-sample. Parameters were chosen on this same 6-year window — low over-fit risk given how few there are, but not a walk-forward out-of-sample test.

Bottom line

The selection logic is validated as far as synthetic data can take it: out-of-sample (110% retention), against baselines (3× the alpha at 40% of the beta), across parameter neighborhoods (plateau, no cliff), net of modeled costs (survives 5% slippage), and decomposed from market beta. Seven of ten acceptance gates pass; none fail. The honest expectation is a modest, real edge — portfolio Sharpe ~0.6, a quarter of months negative, year-long underwater stretches — not a money printer. What remains is exactly what no simulation can supply: months of real quotes (now recording daily, 109 symbols), a paper-fill comparison, and a pilot sized so the worst case costs ~0.2% of capital. Nothing left to compute; from here, only real quotes and time raise confidence.
